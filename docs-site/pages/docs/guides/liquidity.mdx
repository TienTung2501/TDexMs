# Liquidity Provision

import { Callout, Steps } from 'nextra/components'

This guide explains how to provide and manage liquidity in SolverNet DEX pools.

## Understanding LP Tokens

When you deposit liquidity, you receive **LP tokens** representing your proportional share of the pool. These tokens can be burned to withdraw your share at any time.

**Key formulas:**

- Initial deposit: $LP = \sqrt{A \times B} - 1000$
- Subsequent: $LP = \min\left(\frac{\text{totalLP} \times A}{R_A}, \frac{\text{totalLP} \times B}{R_B}\right)$
- Withdrawal: $A_{out} = \frac{R_A \times LP_{\text{burned}}}{\text{totalLP}}$

## Adding Liquidity

<Steps>

### Select Pool

Navigate to `/pools` and find the pool you want to provide liquidity for. Click **Deposit**.

### Enter Amounts

Enter the amount for one asset. The other amount auto-calculates to maintain the pool ratio:

```
Pool ratio: 1 ADA = 0.5 USDT
You enter: 1000 ADA
Auto-fill: 500 USDT
```

<Callout type="info">
You must deposit both assets in the current pool ratio. Depositing off-ratio means the excess of one asset won't earn LP tokens.
</Callout>

### Review Details

- **LP tokens to receive** — Your share of the pool
- **Pool share** — Your percentage of the total pool
- **Slippage** — Price may move slightly between quote and execution

### Sign & Submit

1. Click **Deposit**
2. Backend builds a deposit transaction:
   - Sends both assets to the pool validator
   - Mints LP tokens to your address
   - Updates pool datum with new reserves
3. Sign with your wallet
4. Wait for on-chain confirmation

</Steps>

## Removing Liquidity

<Steps>

### Select Position

Go to `/portfolio` → **LP Positions** or the pool's detail page. Click **Withdraw**.

### Enter LP Amount

Enter the amount of LP tokens to burn. The UI shows how much of each asset you'll receive:

```
LP tokens to burn: 50,000
You receive:
  500 ADA
  250 USDT
```

### Set Minimums

Set minimum amounts for slippage protection. If the pool ratio changes significantly before your TX confirms, the transaction will fail rather than giving you a bad deal.

### Sign & Submit

1. Click **Withdraw**
2. Backend builds a withdraw transaction:
   - Burns your LP tokens
   - Returns proportional reserves to your address
   - Updates pool datum
3. Sign with your wallet
4. Receive your assets

</Steps>

## Impermanent Loss

<Callout type="warning">
Like all AMM pools, SolverNet DEX pools are subject to **impermanent loss**. If the price ratio of the two assets changes significantly from when you deposited, you may receive less value upon withdrawal than if you had simply held the assets.
</Callout>

**When is IL minimal?**
- Stable pairs (e.g., USDT/USDC)
- When price returns to your entry ratio
- When trading fees earned exceed IL

**LP fee earnings:**
- 0.3% fee on every swap through the pool
- Distributed proportionally to LP holders
- Earned continuously, compounding with each swap
- 1/6 of the fee goes to the protocol; 5/6 goes to LPs

## Pool Creation (Admin)

Only the protocol admin can create new pools:

```bash
curl -X POST http://localhost:3001/v1/pools/create \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "assetA": "lovelace",
    "assetB": "abc123def456.TOKEN",
    "amountA": "1000000000",
    "amountB": "500000000",
    "fee": 30,
    "creatorAddress": "addr_test1qz..."
  }'
```
