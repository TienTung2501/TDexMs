## The Intent-Based Model

Unlike traditional DEXs where you construct and submit a transaction directly, SolverNet DEX uses an **intent-based** approach. This means you express *what* you want to happen, and the system figures out *how* to execute it optimally.

## What Happens If My Intent Can't Be Matched?

<Callout type="info">
Your funds are always safe. If no matching or routing is possible, your intent simply remains pending until 
a suitable match is found. You can cancel it at any time to reclaim your tokens.
</Callout>

There are several possible outcomes:

- **Full netting** — Your entire intent is matched directly with another user (best case)
- **Partial netting + AMM** — Part is matched directly, the rest goes through the pool
- **Full AMM** — No matching found, the full amount goes through the liquidity pool
- **Pending** — No viable execution at current prices; intent waits for better conditions
- **Cancelled** — You choose to cancel and get your tokens back

## Understanding Fees

| Fee Type | Amount | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| **Trade fee** | 0.3% | Standard AMM swap fee (goes to liquidity providers) |
| **Netting fee** | 0% | Direct matches between users have no additional fee |
| **Network fee** | ~0.2 ADA | Cardano transaction fee (shared across the batch) |

<Callout type="info">
Because SolverNet batches multiple trades into a single transaction, the Cardano network fee is split 
among all participants — making individual trades cheaper.
</Callout>

## Cancelling an Intent

You can cancel your pending intent at any time before it's settled:

1. Go to the **Orders** page
2. Find your pending intent
3. Click **Cancel**
4. Sign the transaction with your wallet
5. Your tokens are returned to your wallet

<Callout type="warning">
Once an intent is included in a batch transaction and submitted to the blockchain, it cannot be cancelled. 
Make sure to cancel before the solver picks up your intent in a batch.
</Callout>

## Liquidity Pools Explained

Liquidity pools are the backbone of any DEX. Here's how they work on SolverNet:

### Adding Liquidity

1. Choose a token pair (e.g., ADA/TOKEN)
2. Deposit both tokens in the required ratio
3. Receive **LP tokens** representing your share of the pool
4. Start earning fees from every trade that uses this pool

### Removing Liquidity

1. Go to the **Pools** page and select your position
2. Choose how much to withdraw
3. Burn your LP tokens to receive your pool share + accumulated fees
4. Tokens are returned directly to your wallet

### Earning Fees

Every time a trade goes through the AMM pool, a 0.3% fee is collected and distributed proportionally to all liquidity providers based on their pool share.
